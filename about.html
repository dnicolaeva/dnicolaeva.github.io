Hi, my name is Dana!

I am currently working in San Francisco as a software engineer.

I was born and raised in Spain, and yes surprisingly I do dance sevillanas (far right in the picture).

However, my parents are both Bulgarian. And this delicious thing is called banitza.

My first night in the United States was spent at the University of Illinois at Urbana Champaign, where I studied Computer Science.

This means I have a love-hate relationship with airports.

The love part of this relationship is that I get to see this little monster.

I love white bedsheets because they remind me of clouds.

I prefer dates above any other type of food (I might just doubt it a little with cheesecake).

I find arabic architecture ridiculously amazing.

And with a sip of tea is how I begin my day.

When I am not at work, you can find me swimming, hiking, doing yoga and being outside.

My dozen plants are an ok substitution to the puppy my apartment doesn't allow me to have.

On a lazy Sunday you can find me reading non-fiction with a candle on - or watching Archer and drinking a glass of wine.

I fell in love with Barcelona and Gaudi's magic a long time ago.

This is a tiny glimpse of everything I love, but this quotes summarizes everything pretty well:

"Life is a moment. There is no hereafter. So make it burn always with the hardest flame."

-Oscar Wilde
